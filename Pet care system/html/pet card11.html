MY STORY
ELIZABETH is housebroken, crate-trained and not destructive. Even though she does well with large size dogs, she needs a home with no other dogs. ELIZABETH is excellent on leash, walking right by her person and behaves very mannerly around the public. She is a trained dog - she knows “Heel”, “Sit”, “Stay”, “Come” and “Down”. She has done an agility course and loves it.

ELIZABETH was owned by an elderly man who had saved her from one of the high-kill shelters. Sadly, her owner had a terminal illness and died a couple of years ago. ELIZABETH has been looking for a forever home since then.

What type of home does ELIZABETH need?
- A household with or without kids, as she loves everyone.
- Someone who is home a lot.
- A home where she can be an only-dog or with another dog as a companion.

ELIZABETH is very agile and can jump low fences, so a fence of min. 6-ft in height is required.
